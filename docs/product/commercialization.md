# Open-core and commercialization

## Open-source core

The following remain useful without a commercial service:

- Scenario, trace, evaluation, and report contracts.
- Deterministic evaluators and red-team checks.
- CLI and local experiment store.
- Regression policies, CI output, and plugin protocol.

## Potential hosted product

- Shared projects, datasets, baselines, and review workflows.
- SSO, RBAC, tenant isolation, approval policy, and audit retention.
- Managed trace ingestion and provider connectors.
- Trend analysis, failure clustering, alerts, and scheduled evaluation.
- Private evaluator registry and enterprise support.

## Packaging hypothesis

- **Community:** local runner, schemas, plugins, and CI reports.
- **Team:** hosted collaboration, history, scheduled runs, and integrations.
- **Enterprise:** identity, governance, data residency, support, and custom
  deployment.

## Commercial validation sequence

1. Interview 10 AI engineering leads about current release decisions.
2. Observe three real evaluation workflows and quantify manual effort.
3. Recruit design partners using the open-source core.
4. Test willingness to pay for collaboration and governance, not for metrics
   already available in the community edition.

No pricing or revenue claim is approved until customer discovery supports it.
